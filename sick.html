SICK AG

I relocated to South-Western Germany for a four month internship at SICK AG a company that creates incredible sensors. I worked in the light grid division, attempting to take the companies current thirteen light grid sensors and combine them into only three light grid products.

The purpose of the project was to combine six low-cost light grids into one new product that was cheaper, smaller, more durable, and included the key aspects of the six products it was to replace. The main aspects of my position was to design the housing and assembly process for this new light grid. The project was very open-ended and I started using knowledge from university to evaluate current manufacturing processes, competitors products and each of the light grids to be replaced. Working directly with customer and sales data the team determined what features and engineering specifications were necessary for this new product. At the end of my four-month internship a prototype was complete and the manufacturing process was determined for this new light grid.
My time in a German engineering facility has definitely been a key aspect to drive me to where I am today. The biggest takeaways from engineering in that environment is how to justify every design decision. Full simulations, decision matrices, and data was required before any decision could be finalized and ensuring that each of these aspects was a fair test has been incredibly important to my training as a well-rounded product engineer.
