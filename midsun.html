MIDNIGHT SUN

The main project I worked on throughout my Universty career was the Midnight Sun Solar Race Car project building Midnight Sun X and Midnight Sun XI. Midnight Sun is a team born in the 1980's that has built to date eleven solar electric vehicles capable of highway speeds. We have competed in Australia, Canada, and America in the Formula Sun Grand Prix, American Solar Challenge, and World Solar Challenge. I began on the team retrofitting Midnight Sun X for the Formula Sun Grand Prix 2013. Midnight Sun X was our last three-wheeled single seater vehicle and we competed at the Circuit of the America's track in Austin, Texas.

After the completion of this race the team began to design Midnight Sun XI, our first four-wheeled, two-seat solar electric vehicle. This was a completely new challenge for us and was our first vehicle incorporating a carbon fiber monocoque aerobody. I began as a Mechanical Manager on this team and had a hand in design, finite element analysis, testing, manufacturing and assembly of every part of the Midnight Sun XI mechanical systems. I moved on to Project Manager and functioned as the team manager for the Mechanical, Electrical and Business teams.

As Project Manager, I was in charge not only of the team and our progress but also relations with the University, sponsors, alumni and other project stakeholders. We were able to complete Midnight Sun XI in time to compete in the Formula Sun Grand Prix 2016 in Philidelphia. It was an incredible wild ride with a great time and I was able to learn so much from my three years of experience. I could really talk about this for days so if you want to talk about how to build a two-wheel solar electric vehicle that placed as the Top Canadian Car in our class in the 2016 Formula Sun Grand Prix please contact me!
