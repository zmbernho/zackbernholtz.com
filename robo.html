ROBO WUNDERKIND

I am currently the Lead Manufacturing Engineer at Robo Wunderkind a start-up creating modular robotics toys to teach children core programming concepts. I have been leading our manufacturing team in Shenzhen, China and building our supply chain, vendors and manufacturer relationships.

A large part of my role at Robo Wunderkind has been taking initial product designs and building prototypes and performing design for manufacturing and design for assembly checks and changes. If a mechanical component or electrical hardware was unfit for mass manufacturing due to assembly issues, deisgn issues, or reliability I designed an appropriate fix to this problem, then implemented and tested these solutions.

For the mechanical components of Robo Wunderkind I made all parts acceptable for injection molding and worked directly to find our injection molding and tooling suppliers. I work closely with our injection molding and tooling suppliers to ensure that all parts are produced up to our standards and necessary changes to toolings and injection processes were made to produce a finished part within our tolerance specifications as well with the surface finish and function we require.

For the Robo Wunderkind PCB designs I have taken over performing design for manufacturing and design for assembly checks. This involves working with suppliers to source parts, as well as test PCB designs and make the necessary hardware changes to improve functionality to match egnineering specifications. A large cost aspect is involved in the Robo Wunderkind PCBs and components and I work to source and test alternative parts to ensure functionality while reducing BOM costs and lead time for long lead time components.
