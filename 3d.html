PERSONAL PROJECT

During the summer of 2015 I was bored and decided 3D printers are all the rage I would like one. After looking online at options and seeing nothing that fit what I wanted, for the price range I set myself I decided to design and build my own. I spec'd out all of the hardware I would need and designed and machined the necessary mechanical components using a combination of lathe, mill, and laser cutting.

After procuring the necessary motors, Arduino Mega, and other required hardware I assembled and began testing my printer. During this phase I realized some mistakes in the initial design and went about rectifying my mistakes.

I used open-source Marlin software but wanted to include a screen to show sensor data output from the temperature sensor, print time, start time, etc. and needed to write my own code to display these values. Finally, I found my prints were working but they were sticking to my print bed, so I upgraded to a heated print bed and continued tuning the controller to realize more precise designs.
